About Me


Hey there, I'm Justin. I'm currently a third-year Computer Engineering major at Georgia Tech with a primary focus in Software/Web Development and Embedded Systems. I have experience working in both procedural OOP languages and functional low-level languages, not to mention full-stack development experience including HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in the frontend and SQL in the backend. I'm a dedicated and flexible worker who thrives in group settings and constantly looks forward to being challenged.
I'm extremely passionate when it comes to doing what I love to give back and put smiles on faces. Growing up like most people, I was simply a normal kid who had interests. However, my interests didn't start out as interests. Picking up a basketball in 4th grade felt weird because no one on the court around me looked the same. Piano lessons were initially forced into my weeks by my parents. Digital content creation was always intruiging, but felt intimidating in the beginning. However, my interests quickly escalated when I realized they could potentially serve as a personal outlet. I felt a deeper connection with these hobbies as I started expressing myself through unique ways I didn't think were possible. In no time, these activities quickly became engrained into my lifestyle, opened several doors for interacting with different communities, and most importantly helped me find my identity as a person. Read into my experiences to see how my words have manifested into actions.
I spent last summer interning at NASA advancing the inner magnetosphere research area of ional spectral structures and I am currently interning at Lockheed Martin developing software in various areas to meet Navy ship service requirements. I aim to continue using all I have learned in the engineering discipline to make a difference in the world. Read into my projects to see how I've applied my knowledge academically.
